Laboratory for Sensory Neuroscience & Evolution

Research Areas Lab Members Publications Contact
The Himmel Lab combines experimental and computational approaches to study the senses (pain, touch, taste, etc.), from genes to behavior. We seek to understand how sensory systems function, how they evolve(d), and when their molecular toolkits originated (perhaps as far back as the Last Universal Common Ancestor).
The Himmel Lab will open Fall 2026 at The Federated Department of Biological Sciences at NJIT & Rutgers-Newark. The lab is open to aspiring team members of various career stages -- please click here for details about joining the lab.

We study:

Sensory Neurobiology

genes, cells, and neural networks of biomedical, ecological, and evolutionary importance

Organismal Behavior

how life responds to the external world

Twilight Zone Homology

Artificial intelligence and big-data approaches for studying ancient and quickly-evolving gene families
