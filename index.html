About Me

I study insect nociception, the evolution of sensory systems, and mechanisms important to pain. At present, I am a PhD candidate, Brains & Behavior Fellow, and Kenneth W. and Georganne F. Honneycutt Fellow in the Cox Lab.

My current research concerns thermal and chemical sensing in the insect Drosophila melanogaster, and to what degree these abilities are conserved from the last common ancestors of insects and humans (among other species).

I currently mentor undergraduates as part of GSU's NIH funded Initiative for Maximizing Student Development.
