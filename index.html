About Me

In my graduate work, I use techniques in behavioral genetics and systematics to study thermal and chemical sensing in the insect Drosophila melanogaster, focusing on sensory transduction, ion homeostasis, and the evolution of the systems and molecules underlying those processes.

My current projects concern cold and chemical nociception, how nociception functions in drosophilid ecology, and the evolution of nociceptor molecules (e.g TRP channels). We are also interested in how these mechanisms relate to, or can be leveraged to study human neuropathic pain.

At present, I am a PhD candidate and Ruth L. Kirschstein NRSA Fellow in the Cox Lab at Georgia State University. I am also a recipient of a Kenneth W. and Georganne F. Honneycutt Fellowship and a GSU Brains & Behavior Fellowship. I am a daily reseach mentor for GSU's Initiative for Maximizing Student Development, and a writing advisor for the Writing Across the Curriculum Program with the Center for Excellent in Teaching and Learning.

I am also a member of the Preprint Editorial Team at Proceedings of the Royal Society B, where I serve as the team lead for the physiology section.
